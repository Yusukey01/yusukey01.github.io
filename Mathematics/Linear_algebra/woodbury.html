Woodbury matrix Identity

class="theorem"> Theorem 1: Woodbury matrix identity Suppose a matrix \(A \in \mathbb{R}^{n \times n}\) is invertible, also, \(B \in \mathbb{R}^{n \times k}\), \(C \in \mathbb{R}^{k \times n}\), and \(D \in \mathbb{R}^{k \times k}\). Then \[ (A + BDC)^{-1} = A^{-1} - A^{-1} - A^{-1} B (D^{-1} + CA^{-1}B)^{-1} CA^{-1}. \] Note: In the case where \(k << n\), this is much cheaper than computing \((A + BDC)^{-1}\) directly.
Proof: Assume \(A \in \mathbb{R}^{n \times n}\) is invertible and \(B \in \mathbb{R}^{n \times k}\), \(C \in \mathbb{R}^{k \times n}\), and \(D \in \mathbb{R}^{k \times k}\). \[ \begin{align*} &(A + BDC)\{A^{-1} - A^{-1} - A^{-1} B (D^{-1} + CA^{-1}B)^{-1} CA^{-1}\} \\\\ &= I + BDCA^{-1} -B(D^{-1} + CA^{-1}B)^{-1}CA^{-1} -BDCA^{-1}B(D^{-1} + CA^{-1}B)^{-1}CA^{-1}\\\\ &= I + BDCA^{-1} -B \{(D^{-1} + CA^{-1}B)^{-1} - DCA^{-1}B(D^{-1} + CA^{-1}B)^{-1}\}CA^{-1} \\\\ &= I + BDCA^{-1} -B \{(I - DCA^{-1}B)(D^{-1} + CA^{-1}B)^{-1}\}CA^{-1} \\\\ &= I + BDCA^{-1} -B \{(DD^{-1} - DCA^{-1}B)(D^{-1} + CA^{-1}B)^{-1}\}CA^{-1} \\\\ &= I + BDCA^{-1} -BD \{(D^{-1} - CA^{-1}B)(D^{-1} + CA^{-1}B)^{-1}\}CA^{-1} \\\\ &= I + BDCA^{-1} -BDCA^{-1} \\\\ &= I \end{align*} \]
