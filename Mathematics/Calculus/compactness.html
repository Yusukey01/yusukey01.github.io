Introduction

Throughout this curriculum, we have invoked the Extreme Value Theorem: a continuous function on a closed and bounded set attains its maximum and minimum. This theorem is the reason we can speak of "optimal solutions" in constrained optimization—it guarantees they exist.

But what exactly is special about "closed and bounded"? The answer is compactness. In \(\mathbb{R}^n\), compactness is equivalent to being closed and bounded (the Heine-Borel theorem). In general metric spaces, compactness is more subtle—but equally powerful.

Compactness captures the idea that a set is "finite-like" in a topological sense: you cannot escape to infinity, and sequences cannot wander forever without accumulating somewhere. This chapter formalizes compactness and proves why it guarantees the existence of extrema.

Open Covers and Compactness

Definition: Open Cover and Subcover

Suppose \(X\) is a metric space and \(S\) is a subset of \(X\). A collection \(\mathcal{C}\) of open subsets of \(X\) is called an open cover for \(S\) in \(X\) if and only if \[ S \subseteq \bigcup \mathcal{C}. \] If \(\mathcal{C}\) is an open cover for \(S\), then any subset \(\mathcal{A}\) of \(\mathcal{C}\) that also covers \(S\) is called an open subcover of \(\mathcal{C}\) for \(S\) in \(X\).

Definition: Finite Intersection Property

Suppose \(X\) is a set and \(\mathcal{S}\) is a collection of subsets of \(X\). We say that \(\mathcal{S}\) has the finite intersection property if and only if for every non-empty finite subset \(\mathcal{F}\) of \(\mathcal{S}\), we have \[ \bigcap \mathcal{F} \neq \emptyset. \]

Theorem: Criteria for Compactness

Suppose \(X\) is a non-empty metric space. The following statements are equivalent, and \(X\) is compact if and only if it satisfies any one of the following statements.

  1. Open Cover Criterion
    Every open cover for \(X\) has a finite subcover.
  2. Ball Cover Criterion
    Every cover of \(X\) by oepn balls has a finite subcover.
  3. Finite Intersection Criterion
    Every collection of closed subsets of \(X\) with the finite intersection property has non-empty intersection.
  4. Cantor's Criterion
    Every nest of non-empty closed subsets of \(X\) has non-empty intersection.
  5. Cantor's Sequence Criterion
    Every sequence \(\{F_n\}\) of non-empty closed subsets of \(X\) for which \(F_{n+1} \subseteq F_n\) for each \(n \in \mathbb{N}\) has non-empty intersection.
  6. Convergence Criterion
    Every sequence in \(X\) has a subsequence that converges in \(X\).
  7. Spatial Criterion
    \(X\) is complete and totally bounded.
  8. BBW Criterion
    \(X\) is bounded and has the nearest-point property.
  9. Cantor Set Criterion
    \(X\) is a continuous image of the Cantor set.
  10. Attained Bound Criterion
    Every real continuous function defined on \(X\) is bounded and attains its bounds.

Theorem: Extreme Value Theorem

If a function \(f: K \to \mathbb{R}\) is continuous and \(K\) is a compact metric space, \(f\) attains its maximum and minimum. (In other words, every real continuous function defined \(\[0, 1\]\) attains its bounds.)
