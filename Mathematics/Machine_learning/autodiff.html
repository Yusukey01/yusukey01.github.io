Automatic Differentiation

In multilayer perceptrons (MLPs), backpropagation is just an application of the chain rule to compute gradients efficiently. More generally, this technique is known as automatic differentiation (AD). Not just sequential layers, AD applies to arbitrary computational graphs, which is a directed acyclic graph (DAG) representing how variables are computed from input to output.

Example

Consider a function \[ f(x, y) = \log \left((x + y)^2 + \sin(xy) \right). \] we can decompose this into primitives: \[ \begin{align*} &v_1 = x + y \\\\ &v_2 = v_1^2 \\\\ &v_3 = xy \\\\ &v_4 = \sin(v_3) \\\\ &v_5 = v_2 + v_4 \\\\ &v_6 = \log(v_5) = f \\\\ \end{align*} \] Then \[ \overline{v_6} = \frac{\partial f}{\partial v_6} = 1 \] \[ \begin{align*} \overline{v_5} &= \frac{\partial f}{\partial v_5} \\\\ &= \frac{\partial f}{\partial v_6} \cdot \frac{\partial v_6}{\partial v_5} \\\\ &= \overline{v_6} \cdot \frac{\partial}{\partial v_5}\log (v_5) \\\\ &= \overline{v_6} \cdot \frac{1}{v_5} \end{align*} \]
