The Core Idea of Markov Chain

The Markov chain is a fundamental concept that appear in various fields. Although the core idea is the same, the way they are used and studied can vary across disciplines.

In the Markov chain, a system transitioning from one state to another is based on the certain probability. The probability of moving to the next state depends solely on the current state and not on the sequence of event that preceded it.

Let \{X_t\}_{t=0}^{\infty} be the states over time \(t\) then \[ P(X_{t+1} = j | X_t = i, \, X_{t-1} = i_{t-1}, \cdots, X_0 = i_0) = P(X_{t+1} = j | X_t = i). \] This memoryless property simplifies both the analysis and computation of probabilities.
