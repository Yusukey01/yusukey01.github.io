Convergence in Probability

Even in computer science, most of us naturally accept the convergence of probability and distribution based on experimental results, to get deeper understanging of statistics, we introduce formal definition of what exactly convergence means in statistics notion.

Let \(\{X_n\}\) be a sequence of random variables and \(X\) be a random variable defined on a sample space. \(X_n\) converges in probability to \(X\) denoted by \[ X_n \xrightarrow{P} X, \] if \(\forall \epsilon > 0\), \[ \lim_{n to \infty} P [| X_n - X | \geq \epsilon ] = 0 \] or equivalently, \[ \lim_{n to \infty} P [| X_n - X | < \epsilon ] = 1. \]
