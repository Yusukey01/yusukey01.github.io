Bayesian Statistics

In Bayesian statistics, the parameters \(\theta\) are treated as unknown, or random variable and the data \(\mathcal{D}\) are fixed. This is the opposite of the frequentist statistics, which we have seen so far. After observing data, we represent our inference about the parameters by computing the posterior distribution: \(p(\theta | \mathcal{D})\) using Bayes' rule as follows: \[ \begin{align*} p(\theta | \mathcal{D}) &= \frac{p(\theta)p(\mathcal{D}| \theta)}{p(\mathcal{D})} \\\\ &= \frac{p(\theta)p(\mathcal{D}| \theta)}{\int p(\theta ')p(\mathcal{D}| \theta ')d\theta '} \end{align*} \] where
  1. \(p(\theta)\): Prior distribution
  2. \(p(\mathcal{D}| \theta)\): Likelifood
  3. \(p(\mathcal{D})\): Marginal likelifood(or evidence)
