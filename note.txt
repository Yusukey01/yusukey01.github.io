Laplace Transform page (calc-XX TBD)
site does not treat the Laplace transform. The Fourier transform (calc-15) covers most analytic needs in the current curriculum. 
The Laplace transform, however, is the standard tool for:
ODE initial-value problems (\(\mathcal{L}\{y'\} = sY - y(0)\) converts differential to algebraic)
PDE problems on half-infinite time domains (\(t \in [0, \infty)\))
Control theory (transfer functions, Bode plots, stability analysis)
Signal processing (causal systems)
Probability (moment generating function is essentially a Laplace transform; characteristic function is Fourier)

Definition \(\mathcal{L}[f](s) = \int_0^\infty f(t) e^{-st}\,dt\), region of convergence
Standard transform pairs (exponentials, polynomials, trig, shifted/delayed functions)
Operational rules: linearity, differentiation, integration, shifting, convolution
Application to ODE initial-value problems with discontinuous forcing
Application to PDE problems on \(t \in [0, \infty)\)
Optional: inverse via Bromwich integral (requires complex analysis — could be a trigger for 16.1)
