MATH-CS COMPASS Bridging Pure & Applied Mathematics

The Compass Philosophy — Foundations & Mission

Hello, I'm Yusuke. As a developer with a background in both Mathematics and Computer Science, I founded MATH-CS COMPASS to bridge the gap between the rigorous logic of pure mathematics and the practical innovation of computer science.

I created this platform after noticing a persistent divide in the tech landscape: while many in the computer science community rely heavily on mathematical concepts, they often lack a clear, structured overview of how these ideas connect—especially in today's rapidly evolving AI-driven era. Conversely, those deeply involved in pure mathematics sometimes overlook the profound practical applications and innovative potential of their work.

In the modern view, mathematics is the study of structure. While pure mathematicians often prioritize formal rigor and computer scientists tend to focus on experimental results, I believe that understanding the underlying "mathematical physics" of a system enriches both. MATH-CS COMPASS offers an accessible yet principled look at the mathematical blueprints that power modern technology.

The Two Worlds of the Compass

Your journey here is anchored by the Algebraic Foundations established in Section I, which then guide you through two distinct conceptual domains:

  • The Discrete World - Focused on finite structures, logic, and exactness. This domain governs Discrete Mathematics & Algorithms (Section IV), where the laws of integers and graphs underpin everything from algorithmic complexity to the invisible shields of Cryptography.
  • The Continuous World - Focused on change, convergence, and approximation. This is the domain of Calculus, Optimization & Analysis (Section II), providing the "completeness" required for the robust engines of Machine Learning.

The bridge between these realms is Probability & Statistics (Section III), translating algebraic exactness into the language of uncertainty. All paths finally converge in Machine Learning (Section V), the grand synthesis of these disciplines.

Specific tools, libraries, and frameworks may become outdated quickly, but the mathematical foundations are eternal. My goal is to equip you with the insights necessary to not only use existing technology as a "black box" but to understand, adapt, and even create new approaches as the field progresses.

I would like to express my deep appreciation to the students and faculty of the Western Washington University Mathematics and Computer Science Departments. Their passion and inspiration were the primary motivation for creating this platform following my graduation.

Exploring these intersections will support both your academic journey and your professional endeavors. If you have any suggestions, requests, or inquiries, please feel free to use the contact form below. Your input is invaluable in helping improve this site for everyone.

Thank you for visiting, and enjoy your exploration!
